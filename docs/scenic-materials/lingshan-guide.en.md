# Lingshan Grand Scenic Area Visitor Guide

## Overview and history

Lingshan Grand Scenic Area is in Mashan Town, northwest of Lake Taihu in Wuxi, Jiangsu. The approximately 300,000-square-meter site is a national 5A tourist attraction and a permanent venue of the World Buddhist Forum. Its Buddhist history is traced to the Tang dynasty, more than 1,300 years ago. Tradition says that the monk Xuanzang saw a landscape resembling Vulture Peak in India, named the place "Little Lingshan," and asked his disciple Kuiji to establish a Buddhist site here.

Xiangfu Chan Temple received its present name during the Northern Song dynasty. The modern scenic area began taking shape in 1994. The Lingshan Grand Buddha was completed and consecrated on November 15, 1997; the Nine Dragons Bathing complex opened in 2003; and the Brahma Palace and Five Mudra Mandala followed in the third development phase.

## Lingshan Grand Buddha

The Lingshan Grand Buddha is an outdoor bronze standing statue of Shakyamuni. The statue and lotus pedestal are 88 meters high: the Buddha body is 79 meters and the lotus petals are 9 meters. The complete structure is 101.5 meters high and uses about 725 metric tons of bronze.

The right hand forms the fearlessness mudra, symbolizing the removal of suffering, while the left hand forms the wish-granting mudra, symbolizing joy and blessing. The 216-step Cloud Ascending Path is interpreted as 108 troubles followed by 108 wishes. Visitors can climb to the platform, take in views of Lake Taihu, and join the popular "embracing the Buddha's feet" blessing experience.

## Nine Dragons Bathing show

Nine Dragons Bathing recreates the traditional story of the birth of Shakyamuni. The monument is 27.5 meters high and weighs about 260 metric tons in bronze. At its center is a 7.2-meter gilded statue of the infant Buddha on a lotus.

During the show, the lotus petals open, the statue rises and rotates, and nine dragons spray water while Buddhist music plays. The source material records four to five performances per day but does not give a fixed all-season timetable. Arrive early and confirm the day's exact start times on the official notice or at the visitor center. After the performance, visitors may collect blessing water where the site permits it.

## Lingshan Brahma Palace

The Lingshan Brahma Palace, also called the Brahma Palace or Fan Gong, covers about 72,000 square meters. Its five lotus towers symbolize the Buddhas of the five directions. The interior combines Dongyang wood carving, murals, lacquerware, cloisonne, stone carving, glazed art, optics, acoustics, and modern stage technology. Important works include the 28-meter celestial dome, 148 flying apsaras, and the glazed "Avatamsaka World" installation.

The palace is a major venue of the World Buddhist Forum. The "Ode to Auspiciousness" or "Jixiang Song" performance uses a rotating stage, projection, and water mist to present the Buddha's path to enlightenment. The source schedule is 10:35, 11:30, 14:00, and 16:00 daily, with a running time of about 20 minutes. Performance times can change, so the same-day scenic-area notice takes precedence.

## Five Mudra Mandala

The Five Mudra Mandala is a Tibetan Buddhist-style complex of about 5,000 square meters, with red walls, golden roofs, prayer flags, and a surrounding reflecting pool. It enshrines the Buddhas of the five directions and contains approximately 1,500 square meters of hand-painted murals. Visitors can learn about mandala art, walk clockwise around the exterior platform, and turn prayer wheels respectfully.

## Xiangfu Chan Temple

Xiangfu Chan Temple is the historic Buddhist center of Little Lingshan. Surviving features include an ancient ginkgo tree, old wells, and the 12.8-metric-ton Xiangfu Chan Bell, known locally as the "First Bell South of the Yangtze." Bell-ringing is presented as a blessing ritual symbolizing the release of troubles and the growth of wisdom.

## Other attractions

- The First Palm is an 11.7-meter-high replica of the Grand Buddha's right hand and a popular blessing stop.
- The Hundred Children Playing with Maitreya bronze group presents Maitreya surrounded by one hundred playful children and is well suited to family visits.
- Bodhi Avenue links major attractions and offers views toward Lake Taihu and the surrounding hills.
- Manfeilong Pagoda uses Dai Buddhist architectural elements in a landscaped setting.
- Lingshan Retreat offers a quiet garden atmosphere, vegetarian dining, and cultural experiences.

## Nianhua Bay Zen Town

Nianhua Bay Zen Town is included in the guide's Lingshan service area. Main stops include Nianhua Square, Fantian Flower Sea, Xiangyue Flower Street, Nianhua Hall, Wudeng Lake, and Luming Valley. Visitors can combine architecture, seasonal flowers, lakeside walks, cultural activities, dining, and evening light experiences. Event schedules and access arrangements should be confirmed in the current on-site notice.

## Tickets, opening hours, dining, and etiquette

The current source material lists an adult ticket at CNY 210, a concession ticket at CNY 105, a ticket-and-shuttle package at CNY 225, and a separate shuttle ticket at CNY 40. Eligibility in the source includes concessions for eligible minors, students, and older visitors, with free admission for specified young children, visitors aged 70 or above, active service members, and visitors with disabilities. These prices and eligibility rules may change; verify them through the official ticket channel before travel.

The knowledge source does not provide one reliable year-round opening and closing time for the entire scenic area. Seasonal hours, last admission, temporary closures, and performance changes must be checked in the same-day official notice or at the visitor center.

The source mentions a Brahma Palace vegetarian buffet and vegetarian noodle meals, but menu, price, and availability should be checked on arrival. Wear comfortable walking shoes, allow extra time around show venues, keep voices low in religious spaces, follow photography signs, and do not touch statues or ritual objects unless the area explicitly permits interaction.

## Suggested routes

### Classic highlights route, about 4 to 6 hours

Enter through the south gate, continue to the Grand Screen Wall and Bodhi Avenue, watch Nine Dragons Bathing, visit Xiangfu Chan Temple, climb to the Lingshan Grand Buddha, explore the Brahma Palace, and finish at the Five Mudra Mandala.

### Family-friendly route, about 4 hours

Visit Nine Dragons Bathing, the First Palm, Hundred Children Playing with Maitreya, the Brahma Palace, and the Five Mudra Mandala. Keep extra time for the dynamic show and interactive cultural displays.

### Landscape and photography route, about 5 hours

Follow the Buddha Foot Altar, Nine Dragons Bathing, Bodhi Avenue, the Grand Buddha viewpoint, Manfeilong Pagoda, Lingshan Retreat, and Brahma Palace Square. Clear afternoons provide the best chance for Lake Taihu and sunset views.
